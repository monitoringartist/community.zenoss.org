Zenoss Community Alliance (ZCA)

Posted by jmp242 Jul 11, 2011

The community has created the Zenoss Community Alliance (ZCA) which is a group of senior community members who are working to evolve Zenoss core and the community to better serve the needs of the community and the entire Zenoss ecosystem. To this end, the board of ZCA has provided the following agenda:



1) Solidify our position and degree of feelings about Zenoss Inc's neglect of Core
2) Figure organizational model
    a) board makes decisions
    b) democratic decision making via polling
    c) other
3) Discuss pro/con of
    a) separate wiki for documentation/organization and use of Zenoss forums for mass communication
    b) exclusively use Zenoss forums
4) Solidify Core demands/needs
    a) What does Core lack to be a competitive product?
    b) What features does Core need to share with Service Dynamics? - Under the assumption of working coop with Zenoss on Core codebase
    c) What does Core need to operate and compete if forking is our only option in the future?
5) How do we incentivize projects?
6) How do we subsidize incentivized projects?

A conference call discussed many of these points today.

 

These are the action items:

 

1) Set up a mailing list for the board

2) Work out the ZCA "headquarters"

3) Provide more information about all of this to the wider community ASAP.

Problem with new forum breaking edits of longer or older posts

Posted by jmp242 Dec 16, 2009

I'm having a problem where I can no longer effectively edit or add to the existing Community FAQ. The platform will just cut off the last third or so of the document on any new save, no matter what it is. I can't really figure out what that is, but I've reported it, and expect it has been reported to Jive.

 

Anyway, for the Documentors community, I thought I should post why I haven't updated the FAQ recently, and solicit feedback about how to manage this issue, as well as the now pretty lengthy single FAQ page. I think maybe splitting the FAQ by topic might be useful, but then it also makes it... less useful also - the users need to guess if we thought the problem was the same category as they think it is.

 

Maybe moving the "answers" from the main page to secondary pages would help shorten that initial page. This is an OK idea, but a lot of work which will slow updates even more. I'm also hesitant to even try and change the existing FAQ page due to the current fragility of it (I can revert to v11 easily, but that won't help at all with any sort of change).

 

I'm open to comments.
