    Re: Cannot view trap details in Event Console?

    You are going to have to run a transform on them before the details can be displayed.  I do NOT believe that what I am pasting here will make this give the correct data time format, but I think it might transform the characters enough that you might not get the error anymore.

     

     

    evt.enterprises.18070.2.2.2.1.1.1.0 = ":".join(map(lambda c: "%02x" % ord(c), evt.enterprises.18070.2.2.2.1.1.1.0))

     

    Do that for both of the OID's that are giving you trouble.

     

    As an FYI, this was posted somewhere in the forums for people who were getting unprintable characters in MAC addresses.  Thats why I dont believe it will work in desplaying the correct date time values, but should give you printable characters.

    Re: Cannot view trap details in Event Console?

    Are you able to open the event console to view the event summaries and times, or is your event console completely broken?  It sounds like some people are experiencing a different variation of this problem where the event console won't connect at all....

     

    If you can open the event console and view the summaries, but cannot double-click for details then here is how I isolated the problem OID:

     

    Close (the circle with the X that moves them to history) all events EXCEPT the one that is "broken", then logging into mysql and doing:

     

    "select evid from status;" (This will give you the evid for the "broken" event.  If you get more than one result, you have more than one current event)

     

    Then:  "select * from detail where evid='xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx';"  (Your evid goes in place of the xxxx's)

     

    This should give you all the event details and you can spot the lines with funky data and record the OIDs to transform.

    Re: Cannot view trap details in Event Console?

    Andrea - It sounds like you have been able to identify a specific device that causes your problem.  You may be able to learn more by logging into mysql and running:

     

    select evid from status where device='<PROBLEM_DEVICE>' limit 5;

     

    This should give you a sample of 5 Event IDs for that device.  You could then run:

     

    select * from detail where evid='<ONE-OF-THE-EVIDS-FROM-PREVIOUS-QUERY>';

     

    This should give you a full display of all of the trap data, and hopefully provide a clue about which part of the trap is causing a decode problem.
