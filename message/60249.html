    Re: So what's up with all the Spam

    Ok, so let me jump in here with some of my scewed loyalties. Like all good stories, some go astray, and I think that is what is happening here. We are in the midst of reviving the community and the open source nature of the Zenoss Community and Zenoss Inc's culture. No matter how many details I write below, if I were reading this in your seat, I would read it with apprehension and disstrust. All I can say is, the company dropped the rock on community for a number of months. Now, its being noticed company wide how the community is important to us as a company, and how it propels our success. We are getting back in the drivers seat in a major way - and so are the active community members.

     

    I, along with 2 other staff members are actively looking into the spam problem. I am logging dozens a day and sending them to our IT team to delete. This process has been getting back logged for a number of weeks now. The spam issue IS being looked into. It depends on how people are creating the accounts 1) if its automated and 2) if its done manually by real humans or something like Amazons mechanical turk. We have a few ways we can cap off account creation: reCAPTCHA, or account moderation.

     

    Secondly, the ZCA was created initially by volunteer community members to consolidate their voices. Zenoss Inc employees have been attending and taking part, so that we can all have a hand in restructuring this to succeed. Just today, I have posted the begining of some community love. We are bringing to the table a freed KB database with 4 yrs of filtered engineering knowledge. We will be opening edit access to this site that you are on now - so that you and everyone can help to edit this place to be how we all best can use it. Also, our ZenPacks page was getting stale and we will open that up wide as if it were a wiki.

     

    We are in the midst of deciding exactly what future parts of the product will become core, how we can restructure our open source code contributions (github is a favorite), how we might look into better documentation efforts, how we might restructure our licensing, etc, etc.

     

    Keep me informed on your thoughts.

     

    -Nick Yeates-

    Community Mgr

    Re: So what's up with all the Spam

    Tito,

    I am one of those "council members".  I don't work for Zenoss and never have done.  I've been working with Zenoss as an independent consultant for 3 years now, having worked in systems and network management in general for 20 years.

     

    Over those 3 years, we have seen enormous improvements in Zenoss Core - code quality and documentation.  There's still lots of room for improvement, but please don't write Zenoss off so casually.  As Nick Yeates says, Zenoss Inc HAD dropped the ball on Core - but they say they are working on it and we should give them a chance.

     

    Come to that, I feel that the community has also been a bit lacking over the last 12 months - this is supposed to be Open Source - joint efforts.  There haven't been many ZenPacks created or updated recently.  I know its not easy and there have been some stunning contributions, but we shouldn't all just sit back and winge about Zenoss.

     

    The forums is something else that we should all attempt to contribute to - with answers as well as questions.  I am as guilty as the rest of us when things are busy - ask the questions but don't check whether you can answer someone else's questions.  We can't expect Zenoss to answer most of the forum questions - and they ARE getting better recently at answering some of the tough questions.

     

    The Zenoss Community Alliance is an attempt by a few of us outside Zenoss, to get the community working better again - and that has to work so much better if Zenoss Inc is on-side with this effort - which they seem to be.

     

    Enough from my soapbox!  I am supposed to be on holiday.  Back to my alpine paths for another week.
