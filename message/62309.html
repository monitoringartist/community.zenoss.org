    Re: how do you configure zenoss for monitor a computer with 2 operative systems (linux and windows)?

    Modeling in zenoss is the act of going get fetching (mostly) static information from the device. OS type, RAM size, Hard drive size, etc. Modelling happens once when you had a device and then every 12 hours by default.

     

    Is the OS not changing within 12 hours after it was booted? If you want more realtime reflection of the OS type, you could lower the Modelling time. This will effect performance, so be careful. Modeling is IO and network and CPU and DB intense. Watch your resources if you change this drastically.
